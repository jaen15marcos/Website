Function: QuotedSpreads¶

Description¶

The QuotedSpreads function processes a table of market quotes to calculate the dollar quoted spread (dqs) and the midpoint (mt) between the national best bid (nat_best_bid) and the national best offer (nat_best_offer) or bid (bid_price) and ask_price (ask_price). It then calculates the weighted average of these spreads over time.

Parameters¶

  • input.table: A table containing market quote data with the following columns:
    • nat_best_bid : National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • time: Timestamp of the quote.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • input.columns: A list of minimum length 2 and maximum length 3 containing the following column names in the predefined order:
    • nat_best_bid : National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • mkt(Optional): Venue where trade took place, only add if granularity is desired.
  • input.start.time: Start time to be considered (i.e., quotes before this time are not taken into account).
  • input.end.time: End time to be considered (i.e., quotes after this time are not taken into account).

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by sym and date:

  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • date: Date of the quote.
  • dqs: Dollar quoted spread for the given time period.
  • pqs: Percentage quoted spread for the given time period.

Syntax¶

input.table: input.table; input.columns: bid_priceask_price or bid_priceask_price`mkt; input.start.time: 09:30:00.000; input.end.time: 16:00:00.000; result = QuotedSpreads[input.table;input.columns;input.start.time;input.end.time)];

Function: EffectiveSpreads¶

Description¶

The EffectiveSpread function processes a table of trade and quote data to calculate the effective spreads for each trade. It updates the table with the midpoint price, determines the tick test (whether the trade is a buy or sell), computes the effective spread in both dollar and percentage terms, and then aggregates these effective spreads using a volume-weighted average.

Parameters¶

  • input.table: A table containing trade and quote data with the following columns:
    • nat_best_bid: National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • price: Trade price.
    • volume: Trade volume.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • time: Timestamp of the trade.
    • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by sym, date, and listing_mkt:

  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • date: Date of the trade.
  • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • des_k: Volume-weighted average of the dollar effective spread.
  • pes_k: Volume-weighted average of the percentage effective spread.

Syntax¶

input.table: input.table; input.columns: bid_priceask_price or bid_priceask_price`mkt; input.start.time: 09:30:00.000; input.end.time: 16:00:00.000; result = EffectiveSpread[input.table;input.columns;input.start.time;input.end.time)];

Notes¶

  • The tick_test calculation uses conditional logic to determine whether each trade is a buy or sell based on the comparison of trade prices to the midpoint.
  • Effective spreads are calculated and then aggregated using volume-weighted averages to provide a meaningful summary for each symbol, date, and listing market.

Function: getRealizedSpread_and_PriceImpact¶

Description¶

The getRealizedSpread_and_PriceImpact function processes a table of trade and quote data to calculate the price impact and realized spreads after each trade. It updates the table with the midpoint price, determines the tick test (whether the trade is a buy or sell), and computes the realized spreads and price impacts over multiple time intervals (1 minute, 5 minutes, 10 minutes, and 15 minutes). It aggregates these spreads and impacts using volume-weighted averages.

Parameters¶

  • ttnQQ: A table containing trade data with the following columns:
    • nat_best_bid: National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • price: Trade price.
    • volume: Trade volume.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • time: Timestamp of the trade.
    • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • QQ: A table containing quote data used to update the trade data table.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by sym, date, and listing_mkt for each time interval (1 minute, 5 minutes, 10 minutes, 15 minutes):

  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • date: Date of the trade.
  • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • volume wavg drs_k_1m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar realized spread for 1 minute.
  • volume wavg prs_k_1m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage realized spread for 1 minute.
  • volume wavg dpi_k_1m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar price impact for 1 minute.
  • volume wavg ppi_k_1m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage price impact for 1 minute.
  • volume wavg drs_k_5m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar realized spread for 5 minutes.
  • volume wavg prs_k_5m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage realized spread for 5 minutes.
  • volume wavg dpi_k_5m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar price impact for 5 minutes.
  • volume wavg ppi_k_5m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage price impact for 5 minutes.
  • volume wavg drs_k_10m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar realized spread for 10 minutes.
  • volume wavg prs_k_10m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage realized spread for 10 minutes.
  • volume wavg dpi_k_10m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar price impact for 10 minutes.
  • volume wavg ppi_k_10m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage price impact for 10 minutes.
  • volume wavg drs_k_15m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar realized spread for 15 minutes.
  • volume wavg prs_k_15m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage realized spread for 15 minutes.
  • volume wavg dpi_k_15m: Volume-weighted average of the dollar price impact for 15 minutes.
  • volume wavg ppi_k_15m: Volume-weighted average of the percentage price impact for 15 minutes.

Syntax¶

ttnQQ: table containing trade data; QQ: table containing quote data; result = getRealizedSpread_and_PriceImpact[ttnQQ; QQ];

Notes¶

  • The tick_test calculation uses conditional logic to determine whether each trade is a buy or sell based on the comparison of trade prices to the midpoint price.
  • The function calculates realized spreads and price impacts over four different time intervals (1 minute, 5 minutes, 10 minutes, and 15 minutes) and aggregates them using volume-weighted averages.
  • It is important to ensure the time intervals used in the QQ table are appropriately lagged to match the corresponding intervals in the ttnQQ table.

Function: getDepthTrades¶

Description¶

The getDepthTrades function processes a table of trade and quote data to calculate various depth-related metrics for each trade. It computes the bid and ask depth, total volume, total value, average volume, average value, and the proportion of block trades. These metrics provide insights into the liquidity and depth of the market for each security.

Parameters¶

  • ttnQQ: A table containing trade and quote data with the following columns:
    • nat_best_bid: National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • ask_size: Size at the ask price.
    • bid_size: Size at the bid price.
    • volume: Trade volume.
    • total_value: Total value of the trade.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • date: Date of the trade.
    • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by sym, date, and listing_mkt:

  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • date: Date of the trade.
  • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • ask_depth: Total ask depth (sum of ask prices multiplied by their sizes).
  • bid_depth: Total bid depth (sum of bid prices multiplied by their sizes).
  • total_volume: Total volume of trades.
  • total_value: Total value of trades.
  • avg_volume: Average trade volume.
  • avg_value: Average trade value.
  • per_block_volume: Proportion of block trade volume (trades with volume ≥ 10,000 or total value ≥ 200,000).
  • total_depth: Sum of ask depth and bid depth.

Syntax¶

ttnQQ: table containing trade and quote data; result = getDepthTrades[ttnQQ];

Notes¶

  • The ask_depth and bid_depth are calculated by summing the product of the respective prices and sizes.
  • The per_block_volume calculation considers trades with volume ≥ 10,000 or total value ≥ 200,000 as block trades.
  • The function updates the table with total depth and the proportion of block trade volume relative to the total volume.
  • Ensure the ttnQQ table contains accurate and consistent trade and quote data for precise calculations.

Example 1 with Sample Data (Function Code Included)¶

getDepthTrades:{[ttnQQ]
    ttnQQ: select ask_depth: sum raze nat_best_offer*ask_size, bid_depth: sum raze nat_best_bid*bid_size, total_volume: sum volume, total_value: sum total_value, avg_volume: avg volume, avg_value: avg total_value, per_block_volume: ?[(volume>=10000) or (total_value>=200000);volume;0] by sym, date, listing_mkt from ttnQQ;
    ttnQQ: update total_depth: ask_depth + bid_depth, per_block_volume: (sum raze per_block_volume)%total_volume  by sym, date, listing_mkt from ttnQQ;
    select from ttnQQ where date<>0Nd          
    }[joinQQandtt];

Function: getTWAQuotes¶

Description¶

The getTWAQuotes function processes a table of quote data to calculate the midpoint of the National Best Bid and Offer (NBBO) as a time-weighted average (TWA) price. This metric is used as a proxy for the closing price, particularly recommended for thinly traded stocks. The function filters quotes from the last 10 minutes of trading and computes the time-weighted average midpoint price for each security.

Parameters¶

  • QQ: A table containing quote data with the following columns:
    • nat_best_bid: National best bid price.
    • nat_best_offer: National best offer price.
    • time: Timestamp of the quote.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • date: Date of the quote.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by sym and date:

  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • date: Date of the quote.
  • mt: Time-weighted average midpoint price.

Syntax¶

QQ: table containing quote data; result = getTWAQuotes[QQ];

Notes¶

  • The function filters quotes between 15:50:00 and 15:59:55 to capture the last 10 minutes of trading.
  • The mt (midpoint) is calculated as the average of the national best bid and offer prices.
  • The time-weighted average is computed by weighting the midpoint prices based on the time intervals between successive quotes.
  • This method is particularly useful for thinly traded stocks where the last trade price may not accurately reflect the closing price.

Example 1 with MAP Data (Function Code Included)¶ 

  getTWAQuotes:{[QQ]
    QQ: select from QQ where time > 15:50:00.000;
    QQ: delete from QQ where time > 15:59:55.000;
    QQ: update mt:0.5*(nat_best_bid + nat_best_offer) from QQ;
    QQ: update time: `time$time from QQ;
    QQ: select ((next time) - time) wavg mt by sym, date from QQ;
    select from QQ where mt<>0Nd
    }[filterQuotes];

Function: Fragmentation¶

Description¶

The Fragmentation function processes a table of trade data to calculate several metrics related to market fragmentation. These metrics include total volume, total value, the proportion of block volume, and the number of trades. The function aggregates these metrics by date, symbol, listing market, market, and CUSIP, providing insights into the distribution and characteristics of trades across different markets.

Parameters¶

  • f: A table containing trade data with the following columns:
    • volume: Trade volume.
    • total_value: Total value of the trades.
    • price: Trade price.
    • time: Timestamp of the trade.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
    • mkt: Market where the trade occurred.
    • cusip: CUSIP of the security.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by date, sym, listing_mkt, mkt, and cusip:

  • date: Date of the trade.
  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • mkt: Market where the trade occurred.
  • cusip: CUSIP of the security.
  • volume: Total trade volume.
  • total_value: Total value of the trades.
  • num_of_trades: Number of trades.
  • per_block_volume: Proportion of volume that qualifies as block volume (volume >= 10,000 or total value >= 200,000).

Syntax¶

f: table containing trade data; result = Fragmentation[f];

Notes¶

  • The function first aggregates the data by date, symbol, listing market, market, and CUSIP to compute the total volume, total value, block volume, and number of trades.
  • The block volume is determined based on the condition that a trade volume is greater than or equal to 10,000 or the total value is greater than or equal to 200,000.
  • The final proportion of block volume is calculated as the sum of qualifying block volumes divided by the total volume for each group.

Example 1 with MAP Data (Function Code Included)¶

  Fragmentation:{[f]
    f: select volume: sum volume, total_value: sum total_value, per_block_volume: ?[(volume>=10000) or (total_value>=200000);volume;0], num_of_trades: count price by date:`date$time, sym, listing_mkt, mkt, cusip from f;
    f: select  volume, total_value, num_of_trades, per_block_volume: (sum raze per_block_volume)%volume by date, sym, listing_mkt, mkt, cusip from f
    }[filterT];

Function: Shorts¶

Description¶

The Shorts function processes a table of trade data to calculate metrics related to short-selling activity, including the short volume, short total value, and the number of short trades. The function filters and aggregates these metrics by date, symbol, listing market, and market, providing insights into short-selling activity for each security.

Parameters¶

  • filterT: A table containing trade data with the following columns:
    • volume: Trade volume.
    • total_value: Total value of the trades.
    • price: Trade price.
    • time: Timestamp of the trade.
    • sym: Symbol of the security.
    • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
    • mkt: Market where the trade occurred.
    • s_short: Indicates if the trade is a short sale.
    • s_short_marking_exempt: Indicates if the short sale is marking exempt.
    • b_short_marking_exempt: Indicates if the buy side is marking exempt.
    • s_user_name: Seller's user name.
    • b_user_name: Buyer's user name.
    • s_program_trade: Indicates if the trade is a program trade.
    • s_type: Type of the trade.
    • s_market_maker: Indicates if the seller is a market maker.
    • b_market_maker: Indicates if the buyer is a market maker.

Returns¶

A table with the following columns, grouped by date, listing_mkt, sym, and mkt:

  • date: Date of the trade.
  • listing_mkt: Listing market of the security.
  • sym: Symbol of the security.
  • mkt: Market where the trade occurred.
  • short_volume: Total short trade volume.
  • short_total_value: Total value of the short trades.
  • short_num_of_trades: Number of short trades.

Syntax¶

filterT: table containing trade data; result = Shorts[filterT];

Notes¶

  • The function first filters the trades to include only short sales, excluding certain trades based on specific criteria such as marking exemptions, program trades, market maker status, and trade types.
  • It removes duplicates and aggregates the short trade volume, total value, and number of trades by date, listing market, symbol, and market.
  • The result provides a comprehensive overview of short-selling activity for each security.
